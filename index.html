WHAT IS WEB DEVELOPMENT?

Web development is the process of developing websites and web applications that work on the internet. It includes web design, network security configuration, client-side/server-side scripting, and web content development among other tasks.

Let's discuss the Life cycle involved in Website Development

Research & Analysis

The life cycle of web development starts with Research & Analysis. Establish the criteria and assist them in locating the ideal solution. Know your target market and how the website will be used to achieve your business's goals.

If you don't already have one, we will be pleased to assist you in developing one.

Planning & Strategy

This phase involves allocating resources, defining deliverables, estimating timescales, and creating a sitemap.

A complete list of all the website's areas and sub-topics is required. This is a guide that defines the site's content and provides an easy-to-use navigation mechanism.

Design

Choosing the website's appearance and feel is the next process. It is crucial to incorporate things like the company logo and colours that support the website's branding during the design phase.

A designer's efforts result in the website layout. It might be a full graphic design or a sketch. The layout's primary aim is to describe information structure, display content, and illustrate basic functions. Colors, logos, and images are included in layouts, which may provide a general concept of the future product.

Content creation

Writing and compiling content frequently overlaps with other stages of website creation. This phase needs you to write out the substance of what you want to communicate to your website's audience and include calls to action. Content writing also includes developing eye-catching headlines, text editing, new text writing, and assembling existing text. Additionally, your material should be appealing, SEO-friendly, and simple to understand.

Development

The true website construction happens during the development stage. The website's client-side and server-side code are developed at this point. During the website creation process, it takes the longest.

SEO is another essential component (Search Engine Optimization). In order to help your site get higher search engine rankings, SEO involves improving website elements like the title, description, and keyword.

Testing & quality assurance

One of the most crucial elements of any process is testing. After being created, the website is tested. Every single link should be checked to make sure there are no broken ones.Â 

The quality assurance team tests the website's performance, compatibility, and functionality across all devices to ensure that it is prepared for launch. Additional testing may include stress testing, scalability testing, load testing, resolution testing, and cross-browser testing.

Maintenance

After deployment, the web development phases continue. After a deployment, a lot of things need to be finished. As a result of user input, maintenance, and support, several sections have been updated. To maintain the site functioning properly and attracting new visitors, frequent upkeep and changes are necessary.

It is crucial to keep your website up to date.

Always keep in mind that the website development project does not begin with coding and does not conclude once your website is live. Understanding the client's requirements, knowledge about the target audience, market research, planning, design, development, testing, and maintenance are all part of the journey.
